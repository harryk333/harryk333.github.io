I am a PhD student in Astronomy & Astrophysics at Steward Observatory at the University of Arizona. I began my studies in the fall of 2017. My particular area of research is Space Situational Awareness (SSA) with my advisor Dr. Eric Pearce. In particular I develop instrumentation, observing methodology, software, and everything else necessary to track and characterize Earth satellites with telescopes. Read descriptions of my projects here.

My thesis is a comprehensive photometric survey to characterize the brightness of LEO satellites, such as Starlink, and evalutate the impact to astronomy. In particular I am developing metrics to describe the apparent, as-seen in the sky, brightness in a way that is relevant to astronomers.

I previously attended Colorado School of Mines where I completed a double major in Physics and Computer Science. I worked as a research assistant under Dr. Lawrence Wiencke. I conducted research regarding the detection and physical simulation of ultra-high energy cosmic rays in support of the Extreme Universe Space Observatory (EUSO) collaboration.
